<h2 class='date-header'><span>Saturday, June 07, 2014</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://blacknwhitemovement.blogspot.com/2014/06/ang-puno-ng-manggang-hitik-sa-pamumunga.html'>ANG PUNO NG MANGGANG HITIK SA PAMUMUNGA... PINUPUKOL</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-743955863121505681' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;Arial&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;;">By Raul Socrates Banzuela</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;Arial&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;Arial&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;;">Ang puno ng manggang hitik sa
pamumunga...pinupukol.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;Arial&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;;">During the Langkaan Controversy in
1990, the peasant movement was deeply united to defend Butch when he was being
bypassed each time by the powerful Committee on ConfIrmation of Congress.
&nbsp;The public debate then was raging whether or not the piece of prime
agricultural land (Langkaan) in Cavite should give way to Joe Concepcion-supported
Marubeni industry.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;Arial&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;;">Having been among the staunch
proponents of HB 400, which later became the Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Law
(CARL), together with Edcel Lagman and the late Boni Gillego, Butch becoming
Secretary of the DAR after the coup of 1989 and the ouster of Miriam as DAR
Secretary, was received by the peasant and the NGO movement with great
enthusiasm and anticipation of great things to come in the agrarian reform
front. &nbsp;It was no surprise then that even Jimmy Tadeo of KMP who REJECTED
CARP, came to join us in the gallery in the Senate supporting Butch's
confirmation and to witness the gallant defense for Butch's confirmation by
some Senators like Nene Pimentel.&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;Arial&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;;">The rest was history...and tragedy.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;Arial&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;;">After a series of bypass, and an
expected awkward situation in the Cabinet where Joe Concepcion, DTI Secretary,
and Butch Abad, DAR Secretary, would be sitting side by side with President
Cory, he finally succumbed and resigned. &nbsp;It was among the saddest moments
in the peasant movement, feeling a great loss amidst the advance of&nbsp;the
anti-reform forces.&nbsp; Twenty four years
hence, almost all prime agricultural lands in CALABARZON has been wiped out, prime
and precious limited alluvial soil cemented to subdivisions and factories.
Meanwhile the peasant movement has remained in the streets, many of its
leaders, now senior citizens, still pushing, even pathetically just so Congress
would extend the life of CARPER two more years and Notice of Coverage could
still be issued to hundreds of thousands of hectares of land left uncovered, including
the 14,000 hectares of grabbed land (by Danding) in Bugsuk Island in Balabac,
Palawan.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;Arial&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;;">Far from its original vision, a
government centerpiece program not very much unlike that which catapulted
Taiwan, South Korea and Japan to industrialization and modernization, CARP did
not get the Presidential leadership it badly needed (except, perhaps during
Ramos). Some 4 million farmers who received land (some 7 million hectares
reportedly distributed) remained un-attended with the necessary support
services (credit, extension, market, etc.), mostly still living below poverty
line.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;Arial&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;;">Butch, as DBM Secretary, and with
strong support from PNoy, could have the second chance to help ensure funding
availability to support millions of agrarian reform beneficiaries achieve their
historic nation building role.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;Arial&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;;">We just hope and pray, he does not
experience another awkward situation in the Cabinet and succumb the second
time...and resign.&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<br />
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;Arial&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;;">We encourage him to stay put and stand
his ground, and remain united with reform forces in government, and to continue
to join the march of a people who has finally decided to take head-on the
scourge of patronage politics. A new day is dawning...three Senators will soon
be in jail.&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div style='clear: both;'></div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Friday, May 16, 2014</span></h2>

<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<b>THE WOUNDED
PUBLIC SERVANT</b><o:p></o:p></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
Corazon
Juliano Soliman<o:p></o:p></div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;Calibri&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;; font-size: 11.0pt; line-height: 107%; mso-ansi-language: EN-US; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; mso-bidi-language: AR-SA; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-bidi; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-language: EN-US; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;">15 May 2014<br />
<br />
Last April 27th,&nbsp;Sunday, the homily of Fr. Francis really struck me. &nbsp;He spoke about our hesitance to talk about our
wounds and encouraged us to do so.... as Jesus did when he showed and invited
the apostles to touch his wounds... It was not easy to listen as he shared and
he spoke about his dilemma when he wrote a book about the son of a priest, that
it takes courage and humility to talk about our wounds even with our family
members and closest friends.<br />
<br />
It has taken me almost two week to write this reflection piece because I was
afraid that it may be misinterpreted or even derided and twisted to allude
meanings that are far from my own thoughts.&nbsp;
I have been nudged every day since that homily to write, by my
conscience.<br />
<br />
As public servants we live in a fish bowl.&nbsp;
We cannot separate most of our public life from our private life, thus
our family and friends are always affected by public perception -&nbsp;negative
or positive feedback.<br />
<br />
I have known Butch Abad since the 1970's. &nbsp;Together with his wife Dina we were part of
the movement for democracy. &nbsp;He was
jailed together with Dina and their baby Julia in the late 70s because of their
involvement in the anti-dictatorship movement. &nbsp;After temporary release he took up law while
he continued to contribute to the effort of conscientization and mobilization
of the Citizens for Democracy.<br />
<br />
Butch worked with Atty. Hector Soliman, my husband, to initiate and organize an
alternative law practice - as mentored by Sen. Pepe Diokno - wherein people's
organizations were assisted by lawyers who provided legal support during their
struggle for their right to development.&nbsp;
This blossomed and grew into the Alternative Law Groups we know today.<br />
<br />
Butch entered the legislative arena as congressman and he initiated laws:
supported legislation that institutionalized reforms such the agrarian reform
law, and the people's participation&nbsp;bills. Those actions were consistent
with the values and the principles we stood for. &nbsp;Again, as evidence of his commitment to social
justice and truth, whenever faced with a cross road of actions that could
compromise his principles and values, Butch always took the difficult path by
following his conscience - he resigned as Secretary of Agrarian Reform during
Pres. Cory 's administration - a very difficult decision because he loved Pres.
Cory; he chose to leave the Arroyo administration when again truth was
compromised and suffered the consequences of this action such as the
marginalizing of their home province Batanes... Butch has stood for his
principles by taking the difficult path instead of the easy path of
compromising principles.<br />
<br />
When he took up the challenge of being DBM secretary to manage the coffers of
the government, Butch opened up the systems, information and the processes so
that our citizens could track budgets and expenditures, participate in the
budget process and ensure that the funds are spent in a judicious and
responsive manner.<br />
<br />
And yet today he is maligned as the person who taught Napoles the tricks about stealing
via NGOs... I am sure it hurts him, the wounds cut deep and wide... and yet he
will not speak about his wounds. &nbsp;He
continues to do his work, silently bearing the pain even as he tries to show
the public that he is a person of integrity and principle.<br />
<br />
Most of us in public service carry the same wounds.&nbsp; Maybe not as big and deep as those suffered
by Butch, but we all feel that there are many times that we are sacrificial
lambs on the altar of political ambitions of those who may not necessarily be
acting on behalf the common and highest good.<br />
<br />
This reflection piece is for all of us who are wounded public servants.&nbsp; It is good to talk about our wounds and let
our loved ones,&nbsp;family and friends touch them. Their loving touch provides
the gentle healing we need to carry on our mahusay, matapat and magiliw na
paglilingkod sa sambayanan. -- END</span>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>

<meta content='7809178010627845328' itemprop='postId'/>

<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-7809178010627845328' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<b><u>STATEMENT BY SEC. FLORENCIO B. ABAD ON THE SUPPOSED
NAPOLES LISTS</u></b><o:p></o:p></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
"I have never dealt with Janet Lim Napoles or any of
her fake NGOs in any way throughout my career in public service: either as
representative of Batanes, as Budget and Management Secretary, or as an
official of any other public office that has been entrusted to me. I have spent
my professional life&#8212;inside government and in civil society&#8212;working to improve
the lives of ordinary Filipinos by pushing for reforms in public governance.<o:p></o:p></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
&#8220;Moreover, all projects I sponsored under the Priority
Development Assistance Fund (PDAF) when I was a legislator are above board&#8212;none
have been coursed through any fake NGO. Unlike other personalities dragged into
the PDAF Scam of 2007-2009, who have been implicated by evidence and testimony
by whistleblowers, nothing credible has been brought against me &#8211; but rather,
unfounded allegations and malicious name-calling.<o:p></o:p></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
&#8220;I find it amusing, if not outright revolting, that I am
once again in the crosshairs of those who want the Aquino Administration to
fall.&nbsp;<i>Matagal ko nang tanggap na idadawit ako kung saan-saan gawa ng
aming trabaho sa DBM bilang tagapangalaga ng kaban ng bayan at tagasulong ng
reporma sa pamahalaan&#8212;lalo na&#8217;t pinaiigting ngayon ng Administrasyong Aquino
ang Daang Matuwid</i>.&nbsp;<i>Habang lumalapit ang 2016, tiyak na marami pang
lalabas na absurdong alegasyon. Tiwala akong masasala ng publiko ang mga
kuwentong imbento lang, at maninindigan sila para sa katotohanan</i>."<o:p></o:p></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
"Until the public sees the true affidavit of Janet Lim
Napoles, and until hard evidence supports the statements in this affidavit,
everything is speculation, if not total fabrication, at this point. I ask the
public not to immediately believe these allegations, but to weigh the only
thing that matters in the end: the evidence. I urge the Department of Justice
and the Ombudsman to continue their investigation, follow the evidence wherever
it may lead them, and take appropriate action on behalf of the people.&#8221;<o:p></o:p></div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Sunday, December 23, 2012</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://blacknwhitemovement.blogspot.com/2012/12/i-case-you-missed-it-like-father-like.html'>I Case You Missed It - Like Father, like Son? by Yoly Ong</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-102837095762043819' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<br />
<div class="MsoNormal">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
Citizen Y 106 &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;10-16-12 &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;Yoly Villanueva-Ong</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
Like Father, like Son?</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
Just when we were about to forgive-and-forget Juan Ponce Enrile&#8217;s checkered past, he himself reminded us of what a wily, shifty chameleon he truly and naturally is. His stellar performance at the Corona impeachment leveraged enough glory for his son and namesake to become a strong contender for the Senate. Then he launches his autobiography and bio-documentary that attempt to revise history.It&#8217;s almost as if he can&#8217;t help but shoot his own foot.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
And as history and some unassailable sources avow, this would not be the first time Enrile &#8220;shot himself&#8217;. In Juan Ponce Enrile: A Memoir, and bio-documentary &#8220;Johnny&#8221; that aired in ABS-CBN--- he recants his previous recantation of the assassination attempt on him, which Marcos used as one more reason to justify Martial Law.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
&#8220;This accusation is ridiculous and preposterous. What would I have faked my ambush for?&#8221; By that time martial law was already an &#8220;irreversible fact&#8221; since documents had been signed and the military operation to implement it had started.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
&#8220;I honestly did not know why Marcos suddenly decided to cite my ambush in justifying the declaration of martial law when he made his public statement on September 23. There was absolutely no need for it.&#8221;</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
Did he expect national amnesia to afflict Filipinos who know the truth? Are we expected to forget the press conference that he and and then Lt. Gen. Fidel Ramos called on Feb 22,1986 in the Ministry of National Defense when they withdrew their support for the dictator Ferdinand Marcos?In that life-and-death instance, he confessed that the attempt on his life was bogus. The following day, the headlines blared: &#8220;Enrile, Ramos lead &#8216;revolt&#8217; against FM.&#8221; One of the subheads read: &#8220;1972 ambush fake&#8212;Enrile.&#8221;</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
This fact is further documented in various books written by foreign journalists who covered those perilous years. Time correspondent Sandra Burton, wrote in her book Impossible Dream(1989), &#8220;Seasoned observers believed from the start that the attack had been staged. Years later, as he was in the midst of his own revolt from the Marcos regime, Enrile would confirm those suspicions.&#8221;</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
Raymond Bonner of the New York Times interviewed Enrile twice in 1985. In his book Waltzing with a Dictator(1988) he wrote, &#8220;He was emphatic that the attack on him had not been staged, but in February 1986, after he had broken with Marcos and led the revolt that ousted the Philippine president, Enrile admitted that the attack on his car had been faked&#8221;.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
Pulitzer Prize-winning journalist Katherine Ellison also wrote in her 1988 book, Imelda,&#8220;(Enrile) revealed that he had narrowly escaped injury in a spectacular ambush of his car&#8212;an event he conceded in 1986 had been staged.&#8221;</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
In his attempt to leave an acceptable legacy for posterity and bequeath a Senate seat for junior, the nonagenarian is sanitizing his recollections instead of asking for absolution. Stem cell therapy can deter dementia but it cannot regenerate an innocent man.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
And now the gullible electorate is being enticed to continue Enrile&#8217;s political dominion through the son, Jack. By his own narrative, Junior was not close to his father in his younger days. He recalled that as a child, he only watched a movie twice with his dad.The name was a burden for him as he was bullied and beaten up in school for being an Enrile. But today he is closer to his father. &#8220;Mas nag-uusap na kami ngayong ako&#8217;y nasa 50s, and he is in his 80s,&#8221; Jack said. His name is no longer a millstone. He is his father&#8217;s son after all.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
Jack has his own intrigues to expunge. On December 30,1981, actor Alfie Anido, then the boyfriend of his sister Katrina was found dead. The tale turns murky but Jack was seenin the Anidos&#8217; Bel-Air residence inside the room where the body of the matinee idol and a weeping Katrina were found. The official version, which has not been refuted to this day, was that Alfie shot himself.However, the rumor mill tagged Jackie (as he was known then) as responsible for Anido&#8217;s death. This conjecture gained traction and remains an urban legend till now.&nbsp;</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
In his book, the elder Enrile said there was no doubt that Jack&#8217;s supposed role was the handiwork of Fabian Ver. &#8220;No doubt, the only purpose of those who spread that false rumor was to besmirch my family and destroy my reputation rather than to solve a crime for there was no crime at all,&#8221; Enrile said.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
In the documentary, the young Enrile recalled responding to his sister&#8217;s call for help. He said he joined his father&#8217;s chief security aide, Gringo Honasan in Bel-Air.That&#8217;s when he found Anido dead from a gunshot wound to his head. He explained that his alleged connection in the supposed murder of Anido happened because &#8220;I was there. Even the family [of Anido] would say I had nothing to do with it.&#8221;</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
Another misdeed associated with father-and-son is the alleged rampant car smuggling in Port Irene. In 1995, the Cagayan Export Zone Authority (CEZA) was established through Republic Act 7922, authored by Cagayan native JPE. Among the vehicles being sold are Hummers, Porsches, Mercedes Benzes and BMWs, aside from the bestselling vans and minivans. CEZA CEO and administrator, Jose Mari Ponce, swears that the used-car trade was &#8220;very open and transparent&#8221;.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
The flourishing buy-and-sell of secondhand cars in Port Irene &nbsp;prodded the American Chamber of Commerce to submit a letter of concern to then Finance Secretary Margarito Teves. Despite EO156 issued in 2008, which prohibited such importations, smuggling continued. Enrile countered that CEZA is not covered by the prohibition because the importers pay the correct duties and taxes.Ford reportedly pulled out its manufacturing business to protest the nefarious activities in CEZA.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
There&#8217;s a father-and-son story that comes to mind---</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
A father overheard his son pray: Dear God, make me the man my Dad is. Later, the father prayed: Dear God, Make me the man my son wants me to be.</div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
We are being wooed to perpetuate the forty-years-running Enrile saga. Every night we should pray: Dear God, Make all who want our vote, be the men we want them to be.</div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Friday, October 12, 2012</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='17476693' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='6778880337483741360' itemprop='postId'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://blacknwhitemovement.blogspot.com/2012/10/leah-navarro-does-not-represent-urban.html'>LEAH NAVARRO DOES NOT REPRESENT THE URBAN POOR</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6778880337483741360' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<br />
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
</div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; line-height: 115%;">First, there was KontraDaya.
Now, it&#8217;s Anakbayan. Both are part of the ultra-leftist coalition of Bayan and
the Makabayan Coalition. These groups have waged an all-out campaign to
discredit all Party List aspirants not aligned with their Maoist agenda &#8211;
including Black and White.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; line-height: 115%;">Unfortunately, people buy
into their propaganda without bothering to check the facts.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; line-height: 115%;">Before its creation, the
conveners and members of the Black and White Movement were veteran advocates of
various social causes. Undeniably, our nominees do indeed stand for their
sectors.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; line-height: 115%;">They say Leah Navarro cannot
possibly represent the urban poor. In our application for accreditation, we
never said she did. She represents <b><i>women</i></b> &#8211; a sector specifically
defined by the Party List Law as marginalized and under represented. And, why
not? Leah has supported women&#8217;s rights causes even before joining Black and
White. She is an advocate for responsible parenthood and has actively opposed
violence against women. She has represented Black and White at events and
projects supportive of various women&#8217;s causes alongside fellow conveners Karen
Tanada and Beth Yang.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; line-height: 115%;">We wonder why they never mention
our second and third nominees because, like Leah, they represent their sectors.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; line-height: 115%;">Our second nominee is Marco
Cabrera &#8211; a leader of our Black and White Youth chapter. This segment of the
Black and White organization started in 2009 in the Camanava area where it has
been actively involved in engaging the youth via community service projects and
involvement in political issues, focusing on truth, accountability and good
governance. Again, the youth is one sector specifically defined by the Party
List Law as marginalized.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; line-height: 115%;">Our third nominee is Ka Jose
Morales &#8211; a leader of our Black and White Katipunan &#8211; an urban poor group based
in the Taytay-Antipolo area, which has been part of Black and White since its
inception in 2005. A tireless campaigner for the rights of our impoverished, Ka
Jose is a convener of AKKMA (Aksyon sa Kalamidad at Klima), Ugnayang Lakas ng
mga Apektadong Pamilya sa Baybaying Ilog Pasig (ULAP) and the Urban Poor
Alliance (UPALL). Definitely, the urban poor are marginalized.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; line-height: 115%;">While it is true that some
of our conveners are now in the Executive branch of government, it does not
change the fact that Black and White has been actively engaged in advancing the
causes of sectors considered as marginalized &#8211; women, youth, and urban poor. If
given the opportunity, we are committed to protect and advance our multi-sector
interests through responsive legislation.<o:p></o:p></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; line-height: 115%;">We appeal to everyone to
check the facts before buying into these groups&#8217; Maoist-communist propaganda.
Let us not leave the Party List system in the hands of leftist extremists that
are hell bent on blocking the daan na matuwid.<a href="" name="_GoBack"></a> -- END<span style="font-size: x-small;"><o:p></o:p></span></span></div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Sunday, September 30, 2012</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://blacknwhitemovement.blogspot.com/2012/09/the-party-list-system-is-not-exclusive.html'>THE PARTY LIST SYSTEM IS NOT EXCLUSIVE TO LEFTISTS</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-7921431548464188412' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<br />
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-size: 10.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">BnW Media Press Release</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-size: 10.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">29 September 2012</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-size: 10.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-size: 10.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">A leftist, anti-Aquino administration group purporting to be
a non-partisan citizen&#8217;s group named Kontra Daya has called on the Black and
White Movement to withdraw its application for accreditation as a Regional
Political Party participating in the 2013 Party List election. It claims our
close alliance with President Aquino makes a mockery of the Party List system.<o:p></o:p></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-size: 10.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">To Kontra Daya, which is led by Fr. Joe Dizon, a close
associate of the Bayan coalition and Bayan Muna Party List, we have one simple
question: With due respect to Sen. Manny Villar (and the NP, now in coalition
with the LP for the upcoming elections), whom Bayan allies supported in the
2010 presidential election, if he had won &#8211; would Bayan Muna withdraw its
participation in the 2013 Party List election?<o:p></o:p></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-size: 10.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">For the record, ever since our inception in 2005, the Black
and White Movement has always worked with the urban poor sector. They were with
us in our mass actions against the excesses of the GMA regime. In fact, the
lead convener of Black and White Katipunan (our Urban Poor cluster), Ka Jose
Morales, is our third nominee.<o:p></o:p></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-size: 10.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">For the record, we also have a very active and passionate
youth group &#8211; the Black and White Youth Movement and one of its leaders, Marco
Cabrera, is our second nominee.<o:p></o:p></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-size: 10.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">For the record, many of our conveners &#8211; Karen Tanada, Elizabeth
Yang, and Leah Navarro are prominent advocates of women&#8217;s rights and
issues.&nbsp; We have chosen Leah Navarro &#8211;
our first nominee, to represent the women&#8217;s sector. Her current appointment as
MTRCB board member ends on 30 September 2012 and does not conflict with this
advocacy. She cannot be reappointed when our accreditation is approved.<o:p></o:p></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-size: 10.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">These sectors &#8211; women, youth, and urban poor - are
specifically identified by the&nbsp;</span></span><b style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 14px;"><a href="http://www.chanrobles.com/republicactno7941.htm#.UGgBt5jMio0" target="_blank"><span style="color: yellow;">Party List law</span></a></b><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt; line-height: 115%;">&nbsp;as marginalized.</span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt; line-height: 115%;">&nbsp; </span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 10pt; line-height: 115%;">We have meticulously complied with the
requirements for a Regional Political Party (NCR) participating in the Party
List system. All of our nominees belong to the sectors being represented. And
none of them are former politicians. None of them are associated with any
business interest or political clan.</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<span style="font-size: 10.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">WE ARE QUALIFIED TO PARTICIPATE IN THE PARTY LIST
ELECTION.&nbsp; And if elected, we are
committed to sponsor and support legislation that will protect and advance the
interests of the sectors we represent.<o:p></o:p></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 115%;">
<br /></div>
<span style="font-size: 10pt;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">Contrary to what Kontra Daya thinks, you do not
have to be a leftist to qualify in the Party List system and fight for the
marginalized. - END</span></span>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://blacknwhitemovement.blogspot.com/2012/08/encore-bnw-operation-compassion-join.html'>Encore: BnW & Operation Compassion Join Forces</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-8504272775860656138' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">The Black &amp; White Movement and <a href="https://www.facebook.com/groups/288385187875088/" target="_blank"><span style="color: yellow;">Operation Compassion</span></a> joined forces in 2009 during Operation Tulong Bayan. Once again, in the spirit of Bayanihan, we are partnering to augment relief efforts in the wake of the Habagat.</span><div>
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">We have received requests for aid through our chapters in Quezon City, Tondo, Valenzuela and Zambales.</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">We appeal to you for <b>financial donations</b> as it will be easier for our BnW Youth and OC volunteers to purchase ready to eat food, pack blankets and other necessities for distribution. Moreover, some areas within Metro Manila are still impassable to light vehicles.</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">We appeal to you for help that will be much appreciated by our countrymen in those affected regions.</span></div>
